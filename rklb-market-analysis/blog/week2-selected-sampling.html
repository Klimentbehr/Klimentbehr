Selected Sampling

Conditional probability identifies a representation gap between the selected marketing sample and the broader customer population.

The projected marketing sample contains 145 women among 400 people. The broader potential-customer pool contains 510 women among 1,200 people.

P(W | Sample) = 145 ÷ 400 = 0.3625 = 36.25%
P(W | Potential) = 510 ÷ 1,200 = 0.4250 = 42.50%

Figure 3. Women’s representation in the sample compared with the broader potential-customer pool.

The 6.25 percentage-point difference does not prove discrimination. It may reflect occupational demographics, seniority requirements, conference attendance, list-selection methods, regional coverage, or voluntary nonresponse. However, the difference supports reviewing sourcing rules, documenting inclusion criteria, and monitoring segment-level selection rates.
