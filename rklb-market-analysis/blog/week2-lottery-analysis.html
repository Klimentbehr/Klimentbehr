Lottery Analysis

A transparent random-selection process protects the promotional drawing from hidden category or demographic preferences.

A Hawaii trip is awarded to one person chosen from the 400-person sample. The probability of each category is based on its share of the eligible pool.

  • University research labs: 120 ÷ 400 = 30.00%
  • Satellite startups: 110 ÷ 400 = 27.50%
  • Aerospace and defense: 95 ÷ 400 = 23.75%
  • Public-sector mission teams: 75 ÷ 400 = 18.75%

Figure 4. Probability of selecting a winner from each projected customer category.

Fairness requires one entry per eligible participant, written eligibility rules, removal of duplicates, a reproducible random-number process, and retention of the random seed and draw output. If the award is based on merit instead of chance, multiple reviewers should use a standardized scoring rubric that excludes protected characteristics.
