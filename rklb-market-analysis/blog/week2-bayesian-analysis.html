Bayesian Analysis

A Bayesian conversion model updates campaign expectations as pilot evidence becomes available.

The campaign begins with a Beta(3, 97) prior distribution, representing a 3.00% expected conversion rate. A pilot produces eight conversions from 120 qualified leads.

Posterior = Beta(3 + 8, 97 + 112) = Beta(11, 209)
Posterior mean = 11 ÷ 220 = 5.00%

Figure 5. Prior and posterior distributions for the projected conversion rate.

The 95% credible interval is approximately 2.53% to 8.24%. The posterior probability that conversion exceeds 4% is approximately 73.7%. Rocket Lab should expand the campaign only when the posterior probability of exceeding the break-even rate reaches a predetermined threshold, such as 80%.

The model should be updated separately by customer category and acquisition channel. This approach preserves prior knowledge, responds to current evidence, and quantifies uncertainty rather than relying on a single point estimate (Gelman et al., 2013).
